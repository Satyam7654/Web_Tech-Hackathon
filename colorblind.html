Color Blindness

"Color blindness isn't a lack of vision but a different perspective—seeing the world through a palette of shades that others might overlook."

Color blindness is a vision deficiency where individuals struggle to distinguish certain colors, typically red and green. This condition results from the absence or malfunction of color-sensitive cells in the eye, known as cones. While most cases are inherited and present from birth, color blindness can also develop due to aging, injury, or disease. It is more common in males, affecting about 8% of men and 0.5% of women globally. Though there is no cure, tools like color filters and special lenses can help individuals manage the challenges associated with color blindness.
