Common Symptoms

Common Cold
The common cold is a viral infection of the upper respiratory tract, primarily affecting the nose and throat. Symptoms include a runny nose, cough, sneezing, and sore throat. It's usually mild and resolves within a week.
Sneezing
Sneezing is a natural reflex that helps clear the nose of irritants like dust, pollen, or microbes. It’s usually harmless but can be a sign of allergies, infections, or other respiratory issues. Covering your mouth and nose is important to prevent spreading germs.
Flu
The flu, or influenza, is a contagious respiratory illness caused by viruses. It often leads to symptoms like fever, cough, sore throat, body aches, and fatigue. Vaccination, good hygiene, and rest are key to prevention and recovery.
Fever
Fever is a common symptom indicating the body’s response to an infection or illness. It’s the immune system's way of fighting off pathogens. Typically, rest, hydration, and monitoring are essential, but persistent high fever may need medical attention.
Dizziness
Dizziness is a sensation of lightheadedness or unsteadiness, often accompanied by a feeling of spinning or fainting. It can be caused by dehydration, low blood pressure, inner ear issues, or other underlying health conditions.
Rashes
Rashes can appear due to various causes, including allergies, infections, or skin irritants. They often present as red, itchy patches on the skin. It's essential to identify the underlying cause for proper treatment. Consult a doctor if symptoms persist.

Worriers Without Weapons

A Tribute to a Courageous Doctor : While doctors around the world work tirelessly to save lives and provide care, their own safety and well-being are often overlooked. In particular, one tragic incident stands as a stark reminder of the vulnerabilities faced by female doctors. A dedicated doctor, who had devoted her career to the health and welfare of her patients, was subjected to a brutal violation while on duty. This heinous act not only represents a profound personal tragedy but also underscores a societal failure to protect those who selflessly serve us. This doctor’s courage in continuing her mission despite such trauma is a testament to her strength and resilience. Her experience is a call to action for us all, highlighting the urgent need for systemic changes to ensure the safety of our healthcare providers. No doctor should have to fear for their life or dignity while performing their duty. We must honor her bravery by advocating for stricter safety measures and fostering a culture of respect and protection for our medical professionals. This tribute is not just to her, but to all who face risks in their line of work. Let us commit to creating a safer, more supportive environment for those who dedicate their lives to caring for others.
